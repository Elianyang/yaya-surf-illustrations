# Composition Patterns

## Basic Structures

Choose one main structure per image.

### Wave Path

Use for a process, user journey, learning path, launch path, or transformation.

Draw a single wave or shoreline path from left to right. Yaya rides, guides, or marks the route. Use orange for the main direction and blue for water or secondary states.

### Surfboard Bridge

Use for trust, handoff, transition, onboarding, or connecting two disconnected parts.

The orange surfboard becomes a bridge between two islands, two cards, two doors, or two waves. Yaya holds, places, or balances it. Keep labels short.

### Tide Sorting

Use for filtering, prioritizing, choosing topics, triage, or separating noise from signal.

Cards, shells, or floating notes arrive with the tide. Yaya sorts them into 2-4 clear piles or nets. Do not draw a formal table.

### Lighthouse Checkpoint

Use for standards, safety checks, QA, review, evaluation, or direction-setting.

A lighthouse, buoy, or beach sign marks the checkpoint. Yaya compares incoming waves against the signal. Use pink for gentle warnings, orange for the recommended path.

### Underwater Insight

Use for hidden problems, root causes, invisible work, tacit knowledge, or deep research.

Surface waves contain noise. Yaya dives below to retrieve one clear object or insight. Keep the underwater scene simple.

### Before / After Shore

Use for chaos-to-clarity, manual-to-system, stuck-to-flowing, scattered-to-collected.

Left side: messy waves or scattered objects. Right side: calm route, arranged shells, or stable dock. Yaya actively creates the change.

### Floating Workshop

Use for content production, AI workflows, tooling, or maker processes.

A low-tech floating workbench, raft, or dock processes inputs into outputs. Yaya operates one key lever, rope, net, or board. Keep it playful and simple.

### Mini Comic

Use for experience stories, failure-to-success, or emotional state changes.

2-4 small scenes with one action each. The character remains visually consistent. Avoid dense captions.

## Original Metaphor Method

1. Convert the abstract point into a physical ocean action: surf, float, sink, rescue, anchor, cross, sort, ride, dive, dock, signal, collect, dry, launch.
2. Convert the structure into one simple object: wave, surfboard, rope, buoy, shell, lighthouse, tide chart, small dock, floating box, cloud.
3. Make Yaya perform the action rather than observe it.

## Anti-Copy Rule

Do not reuse Ian Xiaohei examples as layouts. The following should be avoided unless the user explicitly asks for an homage:

- black blob character
- hand-drawn black-only machine
- fish cut into multiple uses
- black line pulley/path copied from Ian examples
- broken conveyor belt copied from Ian examples
- "common pits" route copied from Ian examples

Reuse the discipline, not the image.
